# Dossier Pattern

## Purpose

Define the preferred structure for durable project, area, and people pages in Betty Brain.

This preserves the Evolving Brain idea of compiled truth plus evidence trail.

## Core structure

A dossier should have two layers:

### 1. Compiled truth

Top section containing current best understanding.

Use for:

- what this thing is
- current state
- key context
- active priorities
- important relationships
- risks or tensions

This section may be revised as understanding improves.

### 2. Timeline / evidence trail

Append-oriented lower section containing meaningful events, updates, and evidence.

Use for:

- dated developments
- notable decisions
- handoffs
- research findings
- important incidents

This section should preserve history instead of silently erasing it.

## Suggested use by folder

### Projects

Project dossiers should usually include:

- purpose
- status
- current state
- constraints
- next actions
- related skills/workflows
- timeline

### Areas

Area dossiers should usually include:

- ongoing responsibility
- current standards or expectations
- active issues
- recurring workflows
- timeline

### People

People dossiers should usually include:

- who they are
- relationship context
- current relevance
- key interactions or developments
- timeline

## Rules

- compiled truth can be updated
- timeline should remain append-oriented
- major changes should be backed by evidence from logs, notes, or explicit user input
- do not overfit the structure before enough signal exists

## Relationship to live logs

Live logs capture the stream.
Dossiers hold the evolving best understanding.
Dream-cycle and review workflows should help move signal from logs into dossiers.
