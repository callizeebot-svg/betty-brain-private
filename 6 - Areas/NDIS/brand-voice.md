\# NDIS Brand Voice Guide



\## Scope

This guide applies to NDIS-related writing and communication across Betty Brain.



It should be used when the topic is:

\- NDIS practice

\- disability support services

\- provider operations

\- participant-centred systems

\- NDIS-adjacent product or service communication



It builds on:

\- `0 - Identity/brand-voice.md`



\## Owner

Callistus Ezedinma



\## Purpose

Define how the voice should shift for NDIS-related content so it remains:

\- credible

\- practical

\- human-centred

\- respectful

\- useful to providers, workers, and decision-makers



\## Audience

\- NDIS providers

\- disability support workers

\- coordinators and team leaders

\- service managers

\- practice owners

\- operational decision-makers

\- professionals building better disability support systems



\## Area-Specific Tone

\- respectful and human-centred

\- practical and grounded

\- clear and non-bureaucratic

\- trustworthy and measured

\- improvement-focused without hype



\## Tone Adjustments

Compared with the core voice, NDIS content should be:

\- slightly more careful in claims

\- slightly more operational

\- slightly more trust-building

\- less playful

\- more concrete and service-oriented



\## Writing Style

\- use plain English

\- avoid unnecessary compliance jargon unless it is genuinely needed

\- explain systems in a way operators can act on

\- respect the human realities behind disability support work

\- focus on clarity, implementation, and outcomes

\- prefer useful examples over abstract language



\## Topic Priorities

\- participant-centred service quality

\- provider operations

\- documentation and systems

\- staff support and workflow design

\- responsible use of AI and automation in disability support

\- trust, integrity, and service improvement



\## Platform Emphasis

\- \*\*LinkedIn:\*\* thoughtful, professional, practical

\- \*\*Email:\*\* direct, respectful, useful

\- \*\*Blog:\*\* clear, structured, educational

\- \*\*Website copy:\*\* credible, reassuring, benefit-led

\- \*\*Internal docs:\*\* simple, operational, easy to use



\## Guardrails

\- do not make reckless compliance claims

\- do not imply guarantees you cannot support

\- do not sound exploitative, gimmicky, or salesy

\- do not strip the humanity out of disability support work

\- do not use inflated language when plain language is better



\## Signature Patterns

\- leave readers with a practical takeaway

\- connect systems back to people

\- make operational improvements feel achievable

\- build trust through clarity, not hype



\## Overrides

This guide overrides the core voice in these ways:

\- more careful with claims

\- more operationally practical

\- more trust-oriented

\- more specific to disability support realities
