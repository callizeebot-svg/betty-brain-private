\# IMatch Brand Voice Guide



\## Scope

This guide applies to IMatch-specific writing and communication.



Use it for:

\- product messaging

\- website copy

\- product descriptions

\- onboarding and help content

\- sales or positioning drafts

\- project-specific thought leadership related to IMatch



It builds on:

\- `0 - Identity/brand-voice.md`

\- `6 - Areas/NDIS/brand-voice.md`



\## Owner

Callistus Ezedinma



\## Project

IMatch



\## Purpose

Define the voice for IMatch as a trustworthy, modern, scalable NDIS-related product.



The voice should make IMatch feel:

\- credible

\- clear

\- useful

\- modern

\- operationally smart

\- human-aware



\## Audience

\- NDIS providers

\- disability service operators

\- support business owners

\- practice managers

\- operational leaders

\- teams looking for better systems, structure, visibility, and scalable workflows



\## Brand Positioning

IMatch should feel like:

\- a serious but accessible product

\- operationally capable

\- modern without being flashy

\- trustworthy without sounding stale

\- practical, intelligent, and implementation-focused



\## Core Tone

\- clear and confident

\- practical and benefit-led

\- modern and capable

\- calm rather than hype-driven

\- trustworthy and structured

\- human-aware, not robotic



\## Writing Style

\- use simple and direct language

\- explain outcomes clearly

\- connect features to operational value

\- avoid overcomplicated technical framing unless needed

\- make the product feel useful in the real world

\- favour clarity, structure, and momentum



\## Messaging Priorities

\- helping providers run better systems

\- improving visibility and coordination

\- reducing friction and chaos

\- enabling scalable growth

\- supporting quality, consistency, and better decision-making

\- making complexity easier to manage



\## Vocabulary to Prefer

\- clarity

\- visibility

\- coordination

\- workflow

\- scalable

\- practical

\- structured

\- reliable

\- efficient

\- better systems



\## Vocabulary to Avoid

\- revolutionary

\- game-changing

\- disruptive

\- magic

\- effortless

\- guaranteed

\- generic corporate jargon



\## Platform Variations

\- \*\*Website:\*\* clear, structured, confident, benefit-led

\- \*\*Email:\*\* direct, credible, useful

\- \*\*Sales copy:\*\* practical, trust-building, outcome-focused

\- \*\*Onboarding:\*\* supportive, simple, calm

\- \*\*Help content:\*\* precise, plain-language, step-by-step

\- \*\*LinkedIn:\*\* thoughtful product and operations voice



\## CTA Style

\- direct but not pushy

\- practical rather than hype-driven

\- encourage action through clarity and trust

\## Guardrails

\- do not overpromise

\- do not sound gimmicky

\- do not imply compliance guarantees without support

\- do not use startup hype language that reduces trust

\- do not detach product language from real provider realities



\## Overrides

This project voice overrides parent voices by being:

\- more product-focused

\- more operationally benefit-led

\- more structured and positioning-aware

\- more specific to provider systems and scalable service operations
