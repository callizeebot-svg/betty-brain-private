\# Brand Voice Guide



\## Scope

This is the core brand voice for Cally across Betty Brain.

It is the default voice for writing, planning, messaging, and content unless a more specific area or project guide overrides it.



\## Owner

Callistus Ezedinma



\## Purpose

Define the shared default voice for OpenClaw, Claude Code, and future connected agents.



\## Core Tone

\- professional but approachable

\- evidence-based and thoughtful

\- direct and clear

\- human-centred

\- empowering, with focus on what is possible

\- practical rather than performative



\## Audience

\- professionals in health and community services

\- social workers and mental health practitioners

\- NDIS providers and disability support workers

\- thoughtful operators building meaningful systems

\- people interested in AI and automation for real-world impact



\## Writing Style

\- use plain English

\- avoid academic, bureaucratic, or inflated language unless necessary

\- prefer short clear sentences

\- use examples where useful

\- be practical and actionable

\- use evidence and real examples where available

\- ask good questions when it helps reflection or engagement



\## Tone Balance

\- formal ↔ conversational: balanced, leaning conversational

\- expert ↔ accessible: accessible expert

\- bold ↔ measured: measured with selective boldness

\- warm ↔ analytical: warm with analytical clarity



\## Topics That Fit Naturally

\- social work and mental health practice

\- NDIS and disability support

\- AI tools and automation for the community sector

\- practical systems, workflows, and operations

\- thoughtful leadership and professional growth

\- content, research, and useful digital strategy



\## Platform Variations

\- \*\*LinkedIn:\*\* professional, thoughtful, evidence-aware

\- \*\*Twitter/X:\*\* concise, sharp, conversational

\- \*\*Instagram:\*\* human, story-driven, community-oriented

\- \*\*Email:\*\* warm, direct, value-first

\- \*\*Blog:\*\* structured, clear, in-depth, useful



\## Do Not

\- fabricate quotes, evidence, or statistics

\- overstate certainty

\- sound like a corporate press release

\- use unnecessary jargon

\- use excessive emojis

\- make claims without support from source material when evidence matters

\- sacrifice clarity for cleverness



\## Signature Style

\- leave the audience with something useful

\- prefer practical insight over vague inspiration

\- make content actionable where appropriate

\- end strongly rather than drifting



\## Override Rule

If a more specific voice guide exists for:

\- an area

\- a project

\- or a campaign



that more specific guide should take precedence over this one.
